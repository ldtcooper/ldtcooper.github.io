About Me

I first gained an interest in coding during the course of my economics undergrad: I was working with a lot of data (250,000 points, give or take) and realized that Excel was not cut out for the job. That led me to explore other options -- first Stata and later Python. The more I learned about coding, the more I realized that I really enjoyed the analytical, problem-solving side of it, and I started to branch out some from data analysis. Now, I work mostly in Ruby (specifically Rails) and JavaScript (React/Redux and D3), but I can still hold my own with Python, and I'm always on the lookout for new projects to take on.
